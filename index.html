PureLight Halo



The Product

Our product PureLight Halo is an attachment to active streetlights that implementing doesn't require full replacement of streetlights. Being able to be attached seamlessly, It provides seamless integration without overhaul. Our product combines optional adaptive controls (intensity can be adjusted) with passive optical engineering (shield count) to combat the problems of light pollution, glare, and energy waste simultaneously.




The Benefits

PureLight Halo transfigures urban lighting by reducing environmental harm while enhancing public health, safety, and ecological balance. By rectifying the negative impacts of shoddy lighting, the system delivers clear, measurable improvements that help cities become healthier, safer, and more sustainable places to live.

Public Benefits:

Enhanced Nighttime Safety – Improved prominence through glare reduction and balanced light distribution constitutes better reaction time and hazard recognition, making roads and sidewalks safer for drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians.

Reduced Eye Strain and Visual Discomfort – Removes harsh brightness and uncontrolled glare that cause squinting, short term vision loss, and headaches during nighttime travel and outdoor activities.

Better Sleep and Circadian Health – Minimizes blue light wavelengths that suppress melatonin production, supporting healthier sleep cycles for residents living near streetlights and illuminated corridors.

Restored Night Sky Visibility – Reduces skyglow by up to 40%, allowing stars and celestial features to become visible again in urban and suburban areas and reconnecting communities with the natural night environment.

Lower Energy Costs for Cities – Adaptive dimming and intelligent controls reduce municipal electricity consumption by up to 80%, freeing public funds for infrastructure, safety, and community services.

Protection of Urban Wildlife – Shields nocturnal animals, insects, and migratory birds from disruptive artificial lighting, helping preserve natural behaviors and local biodiversity.

Tourism and Economic Opportunity – Creates opportunities for astrotourism and special astronomical events by enabling temporary, coordinated city wide dimming for eclipses, meteor showers, and night sky festivals.

More Welcoming Public Spaces – Comfortable, well designed lighting encourages safe evening use of parks, town centers, and pedestrian areas without excessive brightness or over illumination.

Long-Term Environmental Sustainability – Contributes to carbon reduction goals through long term energy savings while addressing light pollution that impacts ecosystems up to 200 miles beyond city limits.




Safety Regulations

PureLight Halo is designed to meet and exceed industry safety standards, ensuring compliance with municipal, federal, and international lighting regulations while prioritizing public safety and accessibility.

Compliance Standards:

ANSI/IES RP-8-21 – Uses adaptive lighting that maintains mandatory lux levels of 8.0 lux for local streets during the 20% idle state and a minimum of 20 lux in the retrofit's idle state for intersections, highways and major roads.

ANSI C136.41-2025 – Uses a standardized 7-pin NEMA connector for safe 0-10V and DALI dimming control.

FHWA – Uses a 150-foot sensor range and soft transition of changes in brightness for driver safety and visual adaptation.

FCC – The radar sensors we use are FCC pre-certified, specifically the InnoSenT IPM-165.

UL 8750 – PureLight Halo preserves existing wiring by interfacing the existing NEMA socket to prevent fire and shock hazards.

ADA / PROWAG – The photocell module does not protrude into or obstruct the 27–80 inch pedestrian safety zone.

DarkSky International – The shield cone meets the zero uplight requirement and the blue light filter makes it so that the retrofit stays below the 3000K CCT limit.
